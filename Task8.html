    Overview

    When most people hear “chess,” they think of a quiet room, two players staring at a board, and pieces moving at a slow pace. But beyond the 64 squares lies something much bigger: a game that has shaped minds, sharpened strategies, and taught timeless lessons for centuries. Every move in chess comes with consequences. Should you attack, defend, or wait? The board teaches players to think several steps ahead, weighing risks and rewards. This kind of strategic foresight is the same skill successful leaders, entrepreneurs, and problem-solvers use in real life.

    Rules

    In chess, players alternate moves, starting with White, to capture the opponent's King through checkmate. Each piece has a unique movement: Pawns move forward and capture diagonally; Rooks move horizontally and vertically; Bishops move diagonally; Knights move in an 'L' shape; the Queen moves like a Rook and Bishop; and theKing moves one square in any direction. The game ends with a checkmate, a draw, or one player resigning.
